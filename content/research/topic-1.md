## Overview

As a theorist, I use concepts from Statistical Physics, Inormation Theory and Deep Learning to understand various aspects of neural dynamics, cognition and consciousness.

### Self-Organized Criticality in the Brain

According to statistical physics, there exists a space between order and disorder where special proprties such as long range correlations, scale invariance and optimal information processing arise. I have been exploring the brain criticality hypothesis which posits that the brain self-organizes close to this critical phase to learn and adapt optimally. 

### Altered States of Consciousness

ASCs such as sleep, anesthesia and psychedelic states are characterized by profound changes in subjective experience and brain dynamics. I have been analyzing and modeling the information-theoretic signatures that characterize these states.

### Neuro-AI

Inspired by the brain's remarkable ability to learn and adapt, I am also exploring how principles of self-organized criticality and emergence can be harnessed to improve the generalization capabilities of artificial neural networks.
